Omurice

Description

Omurice, a delightful Japanese comfort food, combines fluffy omelette with ketchup-fried rice. Often served to children, it's a fun and flavorful dish that's easy to make at home.

Ingredients

For the Fried Rice

  • Cooked white rice
  • Cooking oil: 1-2 tablespoonsr
  • Onion
  • Chopped cooked chicken
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Ketchup

For the Omelette

  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Salt

Addtional Ingredients

  • Diced Vegtables(peas,carrots,corn)
  • Tonkatsu sauce
  • Soy sauce

Steps

  1. Make the Sauce: In a small saucepan, whisk together some tonkatsu sauce (depending on desired sweetness and tanginess), a splash of soy sauce, and a tablespoon of water. Heat the mixture gently over low heat until warmed through, stirring occasionally. If the sauce seems too thick, add a little more water to thin it out.

  2. Assemble the Omurice: Heat a pat of butter in a separate pan over medium heat. Once melted, swirl the butter to coat the pan.

  3. Cook the Omelette: Whisk together the eggs, milk, and a pinch of salt in a bowl. Pour the egg mixture into the pan and tilt the pan to spread the eggs into an even layer.

  4. Fill and Fold: Once the bottom of the omelette is set and the top is still slightly wet, add your cooked rice to one half of the omelette. Sprinkle the rice with some diced vegetables and drizzle with a little tonkatsu sauce (optional).

  5. Plate and Serve: Slide the omelette onto a plate. Drizzle with additional tonkatsu sauce and serve with a side of ketchup (optional) and Kewpie mayonnaise (optional) for dipping.
